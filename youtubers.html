Game Design:

GMTK: Covers the largest variety of game design topics from game balance, level design, story, pacing, UX, UI, you name it and
there will probably be something about it here Link

Mental Checkpoint: Covers more industry related topics and design practices Link

The Architect of Games: Typically covers genre specific mechanics or designs along with a bit of UX and story analysis/breakdowns Link

Video Game Animation Study: The name pretty much says it all. the channel is all amount animation in video games and looks at
examples of good animation, bad animation and things to consider when animating stuff Link


Game Development:

GameDevGuide: Covers broad aspects of games and how to achieve them in unity such as ui, inventory systems, loading screens and
rendering related things like post processing and shaders Link

Brackeys: There is no way any of you havent heard about his videos, they are fairly old but the information and principles are
still relevant to this day. this will likely be the channel most people will be watching when learning how to do stuff in unity Link

Garbaj: More of a game dev journal about first person shooters and the techniques used to get certain effects or resolve
certain issues. very niche channel and would only recommend to people making first person shooters Link

Dani: Dani isnt too helpful for learning development in my opinion but his channel is a very entertaining way for new people to see
the joys (and pain) of developing video games Link

2kliksphilip: Gets an honorable mention for his videos covering performance, hardware and the tradeoffs and effects of different rendering methods Link

Coco Code: Good channel for learning about ui in unity Link


Game Stories/Analysis:

Leadhead: They break down and look at the deeper hidden meanings in video games stories and the affects a games story can have on
players experiences Link

Games As Literature: Takes a look at the stories of video games and compares them to other methods of story telling. shows
great methods of storytelling that are only or more easily accomplished through video games Link


User Experience and Psychology:

Joseph Anderson: Hands down the most in depth, honest and respectable "journalist" out there (imo). goes in depth to all
aspects of video games and covers multiple points of views such as a mechanic from a developers point of view and also from a players point
of view. It helps to show the transfer of information from developer to player and how despite some things desired intentions they
may come across differently. Because of the depth the video lengths range from 30 minutes to 2 hours on average. Link

Razbuten: Videos are all about UX and their own experiences with different systems Link

FUNKe: formerly a tf2ber recently started covering wider topics about their user experience with different types of games Link

Core-A Gaming: entirely fighting game oriented, covers the psychology behind fighting games and its design Link


Virtual Reality:

RealaryVR: Good channel for some great niche vr tutorials Link

Justin P. Barnett: Channel for some important vr features. showcases some vr assets and is a great place to start Link

VR with Andrew: Good for some tutorials and vr showcases, and shows some great things for all types of vr dev Link

ValemVR: Has some great tutorials for most things need in vr dev, and also has a lot of good devlogs Link
